## Top Findings

1. **Adaptive design achieves 95% fault coverage @ only 3.25× power overhead**
   - Most efficient trade-off across all tested architectures
   - Significantly better than TMR (2.97× for only 67% coverage)

2. **Power scales linearly with complexity**
   - No superlinear overhead from voters or control logic
   - Demonstrates clean architecture design

3. **Activity factor decreases in adaptive mode**
   - Mode-gated control (SINGLE/TMR/PMR switching) reduces spurious toggles
   - Suggests dynamic power management is working as designed

4. **Random fault scenario (S11) creates 40% power spike**
   - Worst-case: 140.5 µW (but still <0.01% of typical FPGA budget)
   - Manageable with thermal headroom

5. **Voltage scaling most effective lever**
   - 0.9V → 18.9% power savings
   - 1.1V → 20.9% power penalty
   - Better optimization target than frequency scaling

## Recommendations

### For Production Deployment

1. **Default to Adaptive architecture**
   - Best power-to-fault-coverage ratio
   - Suitable for high-reliability applications

2. **Implement power optimizations:**
   - Power gate risk estimator in SINGLE mode (+8% savings)
   - Clock gate unused ALUs (+5% savings)
   - Voltage scaling to 0.95V (+10% savings)
   - **Total potential:** ~20% power reduction while maintaining fault coverage

3. **Monitor temperature:**
   - Budget +4% power for 85°C operation
   - Include thermal headroom in worst-case analysis

4. **Frequency flexibility:**
   - Reduce to 50 MHz during low-activity periods (50% dynamic power reduction)
   - All fault scenarios remain error-free at 50 MHz

### For Cost-Sensitive Applications

If power budget is extremely tight (~100 mW):
- Use **DMR** instead of Adaptive (+2.05× power)
- Trade: loss of 1-fault correction, but retain 2-fault detection
- Saves ~40 µW vs. Adaptive
- Acceptable for systems where faults can trigger graceful shutdown
